About Community Code Day

This is an event for people who choose to learn because they can, and build because they love it. Let us take care of the details — just bring your creativity and watch your ideas come to life this April.

Hosted by Youth Computing, BrainSTEM Alliance and the Keyano College Regional Innovation Network, community members are invited to attend this technology conference. The theme this year will be Health & Wellness.

On Saturday, there will be workshops and speakers. On Sunday, there will be more speakers and public viewing. Food, drinks and snacks will be provided.

The Hackathon

For those of you that would like to demonstrate your abilities with technology, you can participate in the hackathon! You will have access to any resources that you will need to create your project and you can have the opportunity to win some major prizes after presenting what you have created!

FAQ

If you are unsure about anything, don't hesitate to email us at, info@communitycodeday.com

Can I attend?

Anyone can attend! Be sure to register! Experience in programming is not required for the conference. Participants can prep by attending the FREE leading up workshops.

How does it work?

Over a 9 hour period, you'll attend different workshops and speaker presentations to learn about coding and how this years theme, Health and Welness, relates to technology! Attendees can also choose to participate in the hackathon and create a project related to this year's theme to win some amazing prizes. We’ll provide you with a bunch of prototyping resources and lots of food to keep you fueled. The following day, we’ll have a judging period for those who compete and public viewing.

How does registration work?

Registration is open until April 26 11:59 PM. Use the registration section on our page to register!

Does this cost money?

NO! This is completely free! There will be presentations, workshops, snacks, drinks, meals, swag, shirts, and a memorable experience! Participants are also eligible to win some great prizes! Sign up for more details about the prizes.

What will I eat?

We’ll provide food for all meals from Saturday’s breakfast and lunch to Sunday’s breakfast and lunch. If you have dietary restrictions, we’ll try our best to accommodate them. Of course, we’ll also have an abundance of snacks throughout the hacking period.

How do teams work if I choose to compete?

Teams are at most 5 people. You can select teammates when you register, or you can register individually without specified teammates. When you sign in on Saturday, a team list will be provided to you.

I'm a beginner. Can I still sign up?

Absolutely! We encourage coders of all levels to come hack and learn! We’ve got many mentors and we really focus on catering to newcomers’ needs by providing leading up workshops. We are extremely beginner friendly!

What's the Sunday public expo?

On Sunday, we will host a public expo where teams can show off their projects to friends, family, professors, corporate sponsors, and anyone else in attendance! During this time, judging will also take place for the attendees who choose to be judged. Anyone not participating is encouraged to stop by to enjoy some snacks and check out all of the cool projects.

How does judging work?

Information about judging will be provided to the attendees who choose to be judged on the first day, May 26th.

What should I bring?

Nothing! Everything will be provided to you. Attendees are encouraged to bring their laptops however, computers will also be available. If you’d like to bring something off-the- shelf that we are not providing, make sure to shoot us an email (info@communitycodeday.com)!

What should I prepare prior to the event if I choose to compete?

It’s a good idea to setup your computer with some development software such as an IDE (i.e. Sublime Text, Android Studio, XCode), a version control tool (i.e. Git). You don't want to spend a large chunk of time downloading software at the event, do it beforehand!

Where is it? How do I get there?

Community Code Day will take place at Keyano College (8115 Franklin Ave, Fort McMurray, AB T9H 2H7). Participants are to meet in the Common Area in the S building facing Franklin.

What about parking?

Parking at Keyano College is paid and the Community Code Day team is not responsible for any parking expenses.

Can my parents come with me?

Community Code Day is a participant-only event from check-in on. However, your parents can surely sign up as a conference attendee and come themselves!

What if this FAQ didn't answer my question?

Please email us at info@communitycodeday.com if you’re in any way confused or concerned! We’d love to help you out :)
