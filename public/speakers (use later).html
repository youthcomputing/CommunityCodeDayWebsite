More About Robyn Villebrun

Robyn Villebrun

Bio:

Hello, my name is Robyn, I am a member of the Athabasca Chipewyan First Nation from Fort Chipewyan, Alberta. I am a passionate RN and entrepreneur. My company is called Medika North, and we offer drug and alcohol testing, health screening, health surveillance and workplace wellness programs. I come into Occupational Health with an extensive background, starting off as an RN in the hub of the oil sands at Suncor Energy in Fort McMurray, and from here my passion grew to care for our workforce where our families and loved ones make a living for us. Medika North’s mission is to support employers in creating healthy employees and safe working environments through quality services, strategic planning, and comprehensive programs.


More About Natali Lavasseur

Natali Lavasseur

Bio:

Natali Levasseur, Registered Psychologist, brings 27 years’ experience in clinical and school psychology to Keyano.  Fluent in French and English, Natali removed barriers to promote mental health and wellness, lead many researched-based, district-wide initiatives, and integrated community services into the public schools for the past 10 years. More About Melanie Galea

Melanie Galea Social Media Marketing

Bio:

Melanie Galea is a very proud Real Estate Professional for RE/MAX La Biche. She has been selling real estate in Fort McMurray for over 10 years and LOVING every minute of it. Her goal is this; she wants her clients experience to be personal, positive and exciting. She embraces technology and the power of social media and videography when it comes to the buying and selling processes.

The passion she has for Fort McMurray and small business has transpired into the Discover Fort McMurray app and Social media following. Encouraging the community to discover, support and shop local.

Melanie sits on the Fort McMurray Chamber of Commerce board and is active with a variety of community organizations and groups. In her free time you can find her working on her golf swing; she has been a title sponsor last year and the Ladies Night sponsor with the Fort McMurray Golf Course for over 10 years.

Recently Melanie has started teaching social media and marketing seminars to local business owners. Sharing with them her insights, tools and techniques to effectively and efficiently market their business or service.


More About Sahr Saffa

Sahr SaffaAI & Creativity: Is Machine Learning an Additive to Creativity or the Silent Killer?

Bio:

Founder of C-Tribe Festival & VP, Canadian Operations of AutonomIQ Since completing his Bachelor of Commerce at the University of Alberta, where he spent five years as a shooting guard and Academic All-Canadian for the Golden Bears basketball team, Saffa has been an active player in the international start-up scene. As a doer, he has a curiosity for finding impactful intersections between commerce, technology, and culture.

As a creative problem-solver, he is very proud of the work done to start the C-Tribe Festival — a community driven by diversity and helping innovative and creative people achieve their dreams. In less than 2 years the festival has grown to five days of innovative & creative programming, sixty speakers, 1000 attendees, and has exceeded $500k of measured, economic impact in 2018. He also works as the VP of Canadian Operations for a San Francisco Bay Area machine learning firm, AutonomIQ, that uses natural language processing, neural networks and search algorithms, and prediction algorithms to fundamentally alter the way software is developed, tested and maintained. In other words AutonomIQ builds robots to test new and exciting software. AutonomIQ currently employs a team of 25 and will be building an additional research and development team of 30 additional people in Canada under Sahr’s leadership.

Mr. Saffa has worked as a international commercial model for more than twelve years with stints in Toronto & New York, and has been featured by major brands & publications that include the likes of Simons, Henry Singer, Kit and Ace, RBC, Forbes, Cosmo Magazine, WatchIt!, Indochino, Travel Alberta, Hugo Boss & Gravity Pope.

He’s a serial entrepreneur and founder of multiple (failed) tech startups, including Cappsule (a video recommendations app), and Crux AR (an augmented reality app). Mr. Saffa sits on the board as the Chair of Communications of the Prairie Sky Urban Gondola project that aims to add modern-day transportation infrastructure in the city of Edmonton. He is currently also a board member of Digital Alberta that works towards growing, supporting, and connecting the digital community. Mr. Saffa is also a Global Shapers Alumni and Horasis Global Vision Community panelist.

Upon graduating university he grew and consulted with a digital marketing agency, Nimbus Creative, for three years after completing his major in Business Law and Economics and a minor in Marketing. In the Spring of 2020, he will be pursuing a Masters in artificial intelligence research. Most proudly, Mr. Saffa is a stay at a home son to two lovely parents and has an older sister he looks up to very much.

More About Krish Shah

Krish ShahChallenge Yourself!

Bio:

Krish is a Grade 10 technology enthusiast and environmental steward who is dedicated to implementing entrepreneurship and innovation in his community. He developed HALO, a smartwatch application that is able to detect and act upon sudden/subtle heart irregularities. This project won a silver medal at the national science fair. Krish is a co-founder of an environmental organization known as EcoYOLO which has brought over 600 students to plant over 4000 saplings around his community. He actively participates in robotics, chess and coding at the provincial level. Krish is also on the board of directors for YouthComputing, an organization that promotes STEM throughout its community. He is a firm believer in diversity and teamwork. Through Krish’s constant volunteering, he was awarded with the prestigious Governor General Sovereign’s Medal for Volunteers.

Krish’s workshop has been created as a means for a team-building exercise and a quick break from the learning and networking that you have been doing all day. This activity is made to challenge you to think outside of the box and shows the value of teamwork as opposed to working alone. This workshop will be fun and entertaining for people of all ages.
